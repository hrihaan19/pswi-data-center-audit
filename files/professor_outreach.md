## Tips for Cold Emails

1. **Don't mass-email.** Send 1-2 per day, customized.
2. **Read at least one of their papers** before emailing them. Mention
   something specific. "I came across your work" is fine; "I read your 2024
   paper on X and found Y particularly interesting" is much better.
3. **Don't apologize for being in high school.** Professors get hundreds of
   emails. Your edge is the work itself; lead with it.
4. **Don't expect a reply from everyone.** Famous professors get hundreds of
   such emails per month. A 10-20% response rate is great for cold outreach.
5. **Follow up once after 2 weeks** if you don't hear back, very briefly:
   "I wanted to check that this didn't get lost — happy to discuss anytime."
   No more than that.
6. **Save responses** — even short ones become valuable references for
   college applications and future outreach.

## What NOT to Do

- Don't claim your paper is "the first" or "groundbreaking" — let the work
  speak for itself
- Don't ask for a recommendation letter in the first email
- Don't attach more than the paper PDF in the first email
- Don't use jargon you don't fully understand
- Don't mention this was your first research paper — it's not relevant to
  whether the work has merit
